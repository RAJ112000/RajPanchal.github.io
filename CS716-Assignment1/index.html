Program Overview

The Master of Science in Data Science program at the University of Regina offers a comprehensive curriculum designed to prepare students for the dynamic field of data science. With an interdisciplinary approach, the program integrates computer science, statistics, and data engineering, ensuring that graduates are ready to excel in a rapidly evolving industry.

Key Features

  • 12-month intensive curriculum: The program focuses on advanced technologies like machine learning, AI, big data frameworks, and cloud computing, providing students with cutting-edge skills.
  • Practicum experience: Gain hands-on experience through a nine-month practicum with top-tier industry partners.
  • Boot Camp: The program starts with a boot camp covering essential concepts like probability, statistics, and programming in Python, to prepare students for the coursework.
  • Data Engineering Concentration: The optional data engineering concentration adds an extra semester and focuses on data engineering skills, including pipeline design and big data technologies.

Unique Characteristics

  • Strategic Location: The University of Regina, located in Saskatchewan's vibrant tech community, provides access to a range of internships and career opportunities in the data science field.
  • Industry Collaborations: The program partners with leading tech companies, including AWS, Google Cloud, and Databricks, ensuring students work with industry-standard tools.
  • Ethical Considerations: The curriculum emphasizes the social and ethical implications of data-driven decisions, preparing students for leadership roles with a strong ethical foundation.
  • Faculty Expertise: Learn from renowned faculty who are actively engaged in cutting-edge research in machine learning, AI, and big data, providing students with insights into the latest trends.

Career Prospects

Graduates of the Master of Science in Data Science program are equipped with the technical expertise and industry exposure needed to thrive in a range of fields, including:

  • Data Scientist
  • Machine Learning Engineer
  • Data Engineer
  • Business Intelligence Analyst
  • Data Analyst

Our graduates are highly sought after in industries such as finance, healthcare, and technology, with opportunities in both private and public sectors.

Admission Requirements

The Master of Science in Data Science program at the University of Regina has the following admission requirements:

  • A Bachelor's degree in Computer Science, Engineering, or a related field.
  • A minimum GPA of 3.0 or equivalent from a recognized university.
  • Proficiency in programming languages such as Python or R.
  • Prior coursework in mathematics, statistics, or data analysis is recommended.

International students must meet English language proficiency requirements. Visit the admissions page for more details.

Program Curriculum

The curriculum for the Master of Science in Data Science includes core courses and electives that provide students with a strong foundation in data science and allow for specialization in areas such as:

  • Introduction to Machine Learning
  • Advanced Statistical Methods
  • Big Data and Cloud Computing
  • Data Visualization and Communication
  • Data Ethics and Governance

Students also have the opportunity to pursue elective courses in deep learning, natural language processing, and data engineering.
